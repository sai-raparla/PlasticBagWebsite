The Persistent Legacy

Plastic bags, like most plastics, are designed for durability, a quality that becomes a curse after their short useful life. They don't truly disappear; they persist for centuries.

A plastic bag or bottle thrown away today could still exist in some form decades from now. That plastic bottle you might have discarded 80 years ago? It's likely still around, perhaps broken into smaller pieces, but fundamentally still plastic. It can take up to 500 years or more for some plastics to decompose.

The Future in 2100

Looking forward to the year 2100, the consequences of our current plastic consumption become even starker. If usage continues to increase, the problems we see today will be massively amplified:

  • The 6th mass extinction is likely accelerating even faster, with plastic pollution acting as a persistent, toxic stressor alongside climate change.
  • The Great Pacific Garbage Patch and other gyres will be significantly larger, choking marine ecosystems and impacting food chains more severely.
  • The sheer volume of accumulated plastic in the environment becomes an almost irreversible burden, much like the challenge of rising sea levels faced by places like Kivalina, Alaska ‚Äì a slow, creeping crisis with devastating long-term effects.

The plastic we create and discard today directly shapes the environmental reality of the future. Reducing our reliance now is crucial to mitigating this lasting damage.
