The Unintended Destination

For countless plastic bags that escape proper disposal, the final destination is often the ocean. Lightweight and durable, they are easily transported by wind and water.

Because plastic bags take hundreds of years to break down, every year our seas become 'home' to more and more bags. They find their way there through numerous pathways:

  • Washed down storm drains during rainfall.
  • Flushed down toilets (especially smaller bags).
  • Blown or washed into rivers, which act as conveyor belts to the ocean.

In fact, it's estimated that over 1,000 rivers globally are significant contributors of plastic pollution flowing into the oceans.

A Persistent Problem

Much like "forever chemicals" known for their persistence and damage to waterways, plastic bags share a similar characteristic: they stay around for an incredibly long time. They don't truly biodegrade but rather break down into smaller and smaller toxic pieces (microplastics).
