A Short Use, A Long Escape

A plastic bag's intended use might only last minutes, but its journey often takes an unintended turn shortly after. Lightweight and easily caught by the wind, many bags escape proper disposal.

Globally, it's estimated that humans use up to 1 trillion plastic bags every single year. Even if disposed of in bins, they can easily blow out during collection or from landfills.

Caught in the Environment

Once loose, plastic bags become persistent litter. They get caught in trees, snagged on fences, and accumulate in ditches and along roadsides, contributing to environmental pollution.
