Raw Material Impact

The creation of plastic bags starts with fossil fuels, and this initial stage has significant environmental costs:

  • It takes approximately 2.2 billion pounds of fossil fuels to make plastic bags in the US every year.
  • The drilling and extraction process can release enormous amounts of chemicals into the air, contributing to air pollution.
  • The overall production and lifecycle of all plastics contribute significantly to greenhouse gas emissions, estimated at 1.96 gigatons of CO2 equivalent globally.

The Manufacturing Process

The transformation from raw material (plastic resin pellets) to finished bag involves several energy-intensive steps:

  1. Polymerization: Raw fossil fuel components are chemically processed into plastic polymers, typically formed into small pellets (resin).
  2. Extrusion: The plastic resin pellets are melted and forced (extruded) through a circular die to create a continuous, thin tube of plastic film.
  3. Cooling & Shaping: The tube of molten plastic is inflated with air and cooled to solidify it into the desired thickness and width.
  4. Printing (Optional): Logos or text might be printed onto the flattened film.
  5. Cutting & Sealing: Machines cut the plastic tube, seal the bottom edge, and punch out handle holes.
